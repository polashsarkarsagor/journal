Foundation

What is Artificial Intelligence?

AI is the simulation of human intelligence in machines — enabling computers to learn, reason, and solve problems.

🧠

Narrow AI

Designed for a single task. Examples: chess engines, spam filters, face recognition. Most AI today is Narrow AI.

ANI
⚡

General AI

Hypothetical AI that can perform any intellectual task a human can. Flexible reasoning across all domains.

AGI
🚀

Super AI

Theoretical AI surpassing human intelligence in every field — creativity, social skills, scientific reasoning.

ASI
History

Milestones in AI

1950
Turing Test

Alan Turing proposes a test for machine intelligence in "Computing Machinery and Intelligence."

1956
The term "AI" is coined

John McCarthy introduces "Artificial Intelligence" at the Dartmouth Conference.

1997
Deep Blue beats Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov — a milestone for narrow AI.

2012
Deep Learning Revolution

AlexNet wins ImageNet, sparking the modern deep learning era with GPUs and big data.

2017
Transformer Architecture

"Attention Is All You Need" introduces transformers — the backbone of modern LLMs.

2022
Generative AI Era

ChatGPT reaches 100M users in 2 months, bringing conversational AI to the mainstream.

Classification

Types of AI Systems

Type 1

Reactive Machines

No memory. Responds only to current input. Example: IBM's Deep Blue responds to a chess board position but doesn't learn from past games.

Type 2

Limited Memory

Uses past data to inform decisions. Example: self-driving cars learn from recent road observations to predict what other vehicles will do.

Type 3

Theory of Mind

Understands emotions, beliefs, and intentions of others. This type is largely theoretical — not yet achieved in real AI systems.

Type 4

Self-Aware AI

Has consciousness and self-understanding. A completely hypothetical category that remains a topic of philosophical debate.
